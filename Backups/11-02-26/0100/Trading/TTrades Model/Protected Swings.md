## ðŸŽ¯ What is a Protected Swing?

**Protected Swing = A swing point (high or low) that holds and is not broken**

It's a confirmed extreme in price that acts as:
- **Validation** of trend direction
- **Stop loss placement** reference
- **Invalidation point** for trades

**Simple Definition:** A swing high/low that price respects and doesn't violate.

---

## ðŸ”„ How Protected Swings Work

### **Formation of a Protected Swing:**

**Bullish Protected Swing (Low):**
1. Price makes new low
2. Series of down-close candles
3. Price reverses
4. **Closes above down-close series** (CISD)
5. New low is now **protected**
6. Stop placed below this low

**Bearish Protected Swing (High):**
1. Price makes new high
2. Series of up-close candles  
3. Price reverses
4. **Closes below up-close series** (CISD)
5. New high is now **protected**
6. Stop placed above this high

## ðŸ” How to Identify Protected Swings

### **Step-by-Step:**

**1. Find the Extreme**
- Look for recent high or low
- Series of candles in that direction

**2. Wait for CISD**
- Price must close beyond series
- Confirms swing is established
- Without CISD = swing not protected yet

**3. Mark the Swing**
- Draw line at extreme point
- This is your protected swing
- Monitor if price respects it

**4. Use for Trade Management**
- Stop below/above protected swing
- If broken = exit trade
- If holds = stay in trade

## âš ï¸ Common Mistakes

**1. Using Unconfirmed Swings**
- Swing without CISD
- Price hasn't confirmed structure
- High chance of being broken

**2. Tight Stops**
- Stop too close to swing
- Normal market noise stops you out
- Give it room (10-20 points)

**3. Widening Stops**
- Swing broken but you don't exit
- Move stop further away
- Turns small loss into big loss

**4. Ignoring Context**
- Protected swing in isolation
- Not considering HTF bias
- Not considering key levels

## ðŸ’¡ Pro Tips

**1. Multiple Timeframes**
- 4H protected swing = Major level
- 1H protected swing = Minor level
- Use appropriate for your trade

**2. Previous Swings**
- Old protected swings become targets
- Price often revisits broken swings
- Use for take-profit levels

**3. Swing Clustering**
- Multiple swings at same level = Strong
- Single swing = Weaker
- Look for confluence

**4. Break and Retest**
- Protected swing broken
- Then retested as resistance/support
- Can indicate trend change
