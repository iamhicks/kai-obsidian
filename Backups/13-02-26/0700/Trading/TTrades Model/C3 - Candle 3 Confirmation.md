## ðŸŽ¯ What is C3?

**C3 = The third candle after a structure break**

C3 is the **confirmation candle** that validates or invalidates the setup. It's the preferred entry point in the TTrades Fractal Model and the foundation of Pete's trading approach.

## âœ… Why C3 is Preferred Over C2

| Factor | C2 (Candle 2) | C3 (Candle 3) |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Timing** | Early signal | Confirmation |
| **Probability** | Lower (~50%) | Higher (~65-70%) |
| **Risk** | Higher | Lower |
| **Wick Size** | Often large | Small wicks preferred |
| **Setup Quality** | Can be false | Validates true intent |

### **The Problem with C2:**
> *"Don't try to trade candle twos that expand a ton because that is when you can get caught in a retracement, consolidation, or reversal."* - TTrades

**C2 Risks:**
- Price may still be finding direction
- Large wicks indicate indecision
- Can reverse into consolidation
- Higher chance of stop-out

### **The Power of C3:**
> *"C3 validates or invalidates the setup."* - TTrades

**C3 Benefits:**
- Shows true market intent
- Smaller wicks (committed move)
- Clearer structure
- Higher win rate

## ðŸ” How to Identify C3

### **Step 1: Find the Structure Break**
- Previous high/low broken
- New swing forming
- Count from break candle = C1

### **Step 2: Analyze C2 (Second Candle)**
**Ideal C2 for C3 Setup:**
- Reversal candle formation
- Price opens, trades aggressively one way
- Then reverses strongly
- Closes near where it opened (indecision)
- **Large wick showing rejection**

> *"We have a nice reversal candle. Price opens, trades lower pretty aggressively, and that is met with an aggressive move higher closing relatively around the same point."*

### **Step 3: Trade C3 (Third Candle)**
- C3 continues the direction established by C2 reversal
- Look for small wick (expansion candle)
- Entry on C3 open or after CISD confirmation
- Target liquidity in that direction

## âš ï¸ C3 Red Flags (Avoid)

**Don't Trade C3 When:**

1. **C2 had massive expansion**
   - Price already moved too far
   - C3 may retrace/consolidate
   - Look for "less than ideal" label

2. **C2 took out all liquidity**
   - If C2 already hit target, no room for C3
   - "If candle 2 would have expanded all the way to this low, that's something I'm not ever really going to trade."

3. **No clear reversal on C2**
   - C2 must show reversal structure
   - Large wick rejecting direction
   - Without this, C3 has no setup

4. **Large wick on C3 forming**
   - Indicates indecision
   - May become C2 of next sequence
   - Wait for next C3
